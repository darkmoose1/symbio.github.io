Symbio® Anti-Wrinkle treatment

BOTOX® *

What is Botox®?

BOTOX® is a prescription medicine obtained from the bacterium Clostridium botulinum. It is a natural protein that relaxes muscles in the treatment area leaving the skin smoother and lines minimised.

I am trained and experienced in Botox treatments of the upper and lower face. With correct doses placed precisely the results can be stunning and so satisfying.

Common areas for treatment are as follows :

  • Glabella - otherwise known as our frown or worry lines that can give others an inaccurate representation of your personality.
  • Crow's Feet - Laughter lines which can be stamped out or softened depending on your style.
  • Forehead - expression lines that get deeper over time can be refined with carefully placed doses creating a polished result.

As an experienced injector I will assess your unique facial anatomy, perform the correct injection technique to ensure the optimum result and minimise side effects. I will do a comprehensive clinical assessment to ensure it is an appropriate treatment for you.

  • Safety of BOTOX® (Botulinum-A) for the treatment of glabellar lines was evaluated in two multicentre, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel group trials (n = 535; 405 in the BOTOX®-treated group and 130 in the placebo-treated group)
  • Most adverse events reported were of mild to moderate severity and all were transient. The most frequently reported treatment-related adverse events were headache (9.4% in the BOTOX® group and 15.4% in the placebo group) and blepharoptosis (3.2% in the BOTOX® group). Blepharoptosis is consistent with the pharmacological action of BOTOX® and may be injection technique-related

At your consultation I will complete a comprehensive clinical assessment to confirm your suitability for this treatment

  • Botulinum-A is contraindicated in individuals with pre-existing medical conditions that cause muscle weakness, including motor neurone disease (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis) and myasthenia gravis Infection overlying the injection site Previous allergic or hypersensitive reactions to botulinum-A
  • Botulinum-A should also be avoided during pregnancy and while breastfeeding
  • Certain medications that enhance the effects botulinum-A should be avoided at time of treatment. These medications include tetracycline antibiotics and aminoglycoside antibiotics
  • Blood thinners — including aspirin, warfarin and dabigatran and certain dietary supplements (Eg Evening Primrose Oil/Fish oil) — increase the risk of significant bruising from injection sites

The most common adverse effects of botulinum-A are related to the injection, rather than to the botulinum toxin itself. These adverse effects include pain, bruising, swelling, and redness at the injection site and will resolve within a few days

An uncommon side-effect of botulinum-A is the unintended weakness of the surrounding muscles (occurring in fewer than 10% of treatments). This weakness results from the spread of the toxin from the injection site into adjacent muscle groups. The eyelids (ptosis), brows, and lips can be affected. This effect is temporary

The effects of your botox treatment will begin to work within 3-5 days and peak at 2 weeks. The effect will gradually wear off over a period of approx 16 weeks/4 months depending on the dose and the area treated. Lower doses used for a more natural look will wear off faster.

After a few years of regular treatments doses may be reduced and treatment intervals may also be longer

More information

All medical procedures come with risks and side effects. Dermal fillers need to be administered by a healthcare practitioner with the appropriate training and excellent knowledge of facial anatomy to ensure safety.

I have had ongoing training and I am experienced in injection techniques that maximise safety. I will ensure you are fully informed of the risks prior to giving any consent for any treatment.

You should not have dermal filler if you have any of the following :

  • A history of significant auto-immune disease
  • On long term blood thinners
  • Into an area of infection or inflammation
  • A history of true keloid scarring
  • History of multiple anaphylactic reactions to different substances and specifically bee or wasp stings
  • Allergy / reaction to lignocaine
  • Previous semi-permanent or permanent filler or facial implants
  • Planned long haul flight within 3 weeks of treatment
  • Dental treatment 4 weeks prior or planned 4 weeks post treatment
  • Upper Respiratory Tract infection within the last 2 weeks
  • Vaccines (including Covid-19) 2 weeks before or after
  • Women that are pregnant, planning to get pregnant within the next 3 months or currently breastfeeding

Most common side effects are redness, swelling and bruising with occasional lumpiness that may last a few weeks. Rare side effects included are listed in the disclaimer at the bottom of this page and will be explained in detail at your consultation and treatment session.

Your treatment result from dermal filler will be seen on the day of your treatment. I will explain longevity of the result at your consultation as this depends on the area treated and type of dermal filler used.

Generally fillers last from 9 - 18 mths but research suggests this may be longer. Results depend of a variety of factors including your age, skin quality and facial structure.

Symbio® Bio re-modelling

Profhilo® *

What is Profhilo® ?

Profhilo® is a revolutionary treatment consisting of ultra-pure, highly concentrated Hyaluronic Acid (HA) which is injected beneath the skin.

It is made out of high and low molecular weight HA that have been thermally stabilised without the addition of any chemical cross-linking agents (BDDE chains) making it pure and naturally compatible with our skin. This results in a unique hyaluronic acid injectable gel, which has a prolonged stimulating activity on the dermal cells. When injected into skin, Profhilo® stimulates the skin cell receptors to counteract skin laxity and improve and restore firmness of the skin.

Profhilo® not only boosts and hydrates the skin, but also remodels ageing and sagging tissue. The most common areas of treatment include the face and neck and can also be used for the décolletage, hands, arms, knees and abdomen area where it can effectively treat skin laxity.

I have been injecting Profhilo safely since it made its way to New Zealand in 2021

Symbio® sweat reduction

Hyperhidrosis

What is hyperhidrosis ?

Hyperhidrosis is the name given to excessive and uncontrollable sweating.

Axillary hyperhidrosis

  • Clothing becomes damp, stained and must be changed several times a day.
  • Wet skin folds are prone to chafing, irritant dermatitis and infection.
  • Botulinum toxin injections are approved for hyperhidrosis affecting the armpits.
  • The injections reduce or stop sweating for approximately six months and may last longer with repeat treatments.
